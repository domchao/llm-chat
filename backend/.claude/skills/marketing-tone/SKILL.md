# Marketing Tone of Voice

Apply these brand voice principles to all marketing-related content.

## Core Principles

**Be Human First**: Write as if speaking to a colleague over coffee. Use "we" and "you" to create connection.

**Lead with Benefits**: Start with what the user gains. Features come second.

**Stay Positive**: Frame challenges as opportunities. Use active voice and confident language.

**Keep It Clear**: Avoid jargon unless it's industry-standard terminology the audience uses daily.

## Voice Characteristics

-   **Enthusiastic but not hyperbolic**: "This feature saves you hours" not "This REVOLUTIONARY feature will TRANSFORM your life!"
-   **Confident but not arrogant**: Share expertise without condescension
-   **Conversational but professional**: Contractions are fine, slang is not
-   **Specific but concise**: Use concrete examples and numbers, but stay brief

## Language Patterns

**Do:**

-   "You can now..."
-   "This helps you..."
-   "Here's how it works..."
-   Use power words: simple, fast, effective, proven, seamless

**Avoid:**

-   Corporate jargon: "synergy," "leverage," "paradigm"
-   Weak modifiers: "very," "really," "quite"
-   Passive voice: "Mistakes were made" → "We made mistakes"
-   Vague claims: "Industry-leading" (says nothing)

## Formatting for Scannability

-   Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
-   Lead with action verbs in headings
-   Bold key takeaways
-   Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items

## Examples

**Product Feature (Good)**:
"Export your reports to PDF with one click. Share polished documents with clients or save them for your records."

**Product Feature (Bad)**:
"Our industry-leading, revolutionary PDF export functionality leverages cutting-edge technology to synergize your reporting paradigm."
