# Q4 Marketing Campaign Analysis

**Date**: 2024-01-15
**Author**: Marketing Team

## Executive Summary

-   Campaign exceeded engagement targets by 34% but underperformed on conversions
-   Email channel drove 68% of qualified leads despite being 22% of budget
-   Recommendation: Reallocate budget from social to email for Q1

**Critical Action**: Pause underperforming Instagram ads and redirect $15K to email campaigns by Jan 30.

## Methodology

-   Data source: Google Analytics, HubSpot, social media analytics
-   Period: Oct 1 - Dec 31, 2024
-   Channels analyzed: Email, social media (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn), paid search
-   Limitation: Attribution model uses last-click, may undervalue awareness channels

## Detailed Findings

### Email Marketing: Strong Performer

-   **Open rate**: 28.4% (↑12% vs Q3)
-   **Click rate**: 4.2% (↑8% vs Q3)
-   **Conversions**: 342 (68% of total)
-   **Cost per conversion**: $24

### Social Media: Mixed Results

-   **Facebook**: Positive ROI, 89 conversions at $41/conversion
-   **Instagram**: Negative ROI, 12 conversions at $127/conversion
-   **LinkedIn**: Break-even, 43 conversions at $68/conversion

### Paid Search: Steady Performance

-   Maintained targets
-   156 conversions at $38/conversion

## Recommendations

**High Priority**:

1. Pause Instagram campaign immediately (bleeding budget)
2. Increase email frequency from 1x to 2x weekly
3. Allocate Instagram budget ($15K) to email tests

**Medium Priority**:

1. A/B test LinkedIn ad creative to improve cost/conversion
2. Expand successful Facebook audiences

**Low Priority**:

1. Test Instagram Reels format in Q2 with small budget

## Conclusion

Q4 campaign revealed email as our strongest channel. By reallocating budget from Instagram to email and increasing frequency, we project 25% increase in Q1 conversions while reducing overall cost per conversion.

**Next Steps**: Budget reallocation by Jan 30, email ramp-up by Feb 5.
